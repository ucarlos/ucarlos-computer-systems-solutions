A single byte can be represented by 2 hexadecimal digits. Fill in the missing
entries in the following table, giving the decimal, binary, and hexadecimal
values of different byte patterns:



Decimal		Binary		Hexadecimal
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
0		0000 0000	0x00
158		1001 1110    	0x9e
76		0100 1010	0x4a
145		1001 0001	0x91
174		1010 1110	0xae
60		0011 1100 	0x3c
241		1111 0001	0xf1

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Work:

16 * 9 = 90 + 54 = 144
158 = 9 * 16 + 14 -->e
9 = 0 * 16 + 9

76 = 4 * 16 + a
4  = 0 * 16 + 4


145 = 9 * 16 + 1
9 = 0 * 16 + 9


10 * 16 + 14 = 160 + 14 = 174

3 * 16 + 12 = 48 + 12 = 60

15 * 16 + 1 == 240 + 1 = 241
